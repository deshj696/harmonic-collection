THE SUNDAY CHRONICLES

still dreaming

sunday started slow. i woke up still thinking about the sunset i saw at pebble beach in dumbo the evening before. that spot always feels like a pause button. it’s quiet, soft, and makes me forget how loud the city can be. on saturday, i stopped by foot locker for some retail therapy and finally got the burgundy sneakers i wanted. they made me happy. i spent the night in brooklyn listening to my playlist that carried into sunday. by the afternoon, i had them on my feet, walking through central park with no real plan, just letting the day move at its own pace.

witnessing love

the air was cold and cloudy when i walked through sheep meadow. i had an iced banana bread latte in my hand, even though it felt more like a tea day. i found a bench near bethesda fountain and sat there for a while. two ducks were splashing in the pond, chasing each other, and for a moment it looked like they were in love. then a couple in a boat drifted into view, dressed for a wedding shoot. both the ducks and the couple shared the same scene for a second, and it felt like a small coincidence that meant something. a quiet reminder that love finds its way around us all the time.

happy birthday baba

it was my dad’s birthday a few days ago, but he was away on a work trip. when he got back, me and my sister took our parents out for dinner and brought him a small cake. we went to a korean restaurant called take 31 that had really good food. the seafood udon was perfect, they gave us tteokbokki, and the corn cheese pancake was unreal. baba, that’s what i call my dad, looked happy the whole time. we talked, laughed, and watched him enjoy every bite. it was simple, and it felt right.
