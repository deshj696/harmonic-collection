The Sunday Chronicles

Kenka Sundays

favorite japanese izakaya

It was the second month of freshman year when me and my friends found this izakaya called Kenka near St Marks. It had vintage interior, dim lighting and really cool Japanese posters. It had loud conversations, a cotton candy machine and really good food; we were sold. This was our spot.

girls night out

So off we went to our favorite place on a Sunday night. They have a waitlist sheet outside you're supposed to put your name down on, and the lady who works there is ruthless with it. If you miss your name being called out, she scratches off your name within seconds. We were seated within half an hour, which was definitely not the worst amount of time we've waited for a bowl of their steaming hot udon.

7$ udon in manhattan?

Speaking of this heavenly udon, that's not the only thing we get. $5 cheese harumaki which is basically mozzie sticks but better, $8 calamari and literally anything our heart desires. I think I would solely go for a cold glass of some calpico. We ate and drank and laughed our hearts out, as we got ready for another week of schoolwork. Being a sophomore makes me reminisce on when we used to come here as freshman, all walking to the restaurant from the same dorm. Now, it is bittersweet as we gather their on Sunday evenings, all from different homes to our home.
